Seminar 5

I have chosen Sina Weibo from China, for the data breach case study. Although I did not present, but I have prepared the responses of the questions:

Q: What types of data were affected?

A: Users’ all real information, including names, gender, phone numbers, email addresses and locations, such as where they are working and living.

Q: What happened?

A: Every personal data was leaked and collected by third party and being sold in the underground market with check price, from £200 up.

Q: Who was responsible?

A: Never mentioned. In fact, you won’t know who needed to be responsible and everything is covered inside China.

Q: Was the Business Continuity Plan instigated?

A: Nothing mention due to the blockage of information inside China.

Q: Was the ICO notified?

A: Again, no information about this.

Q: Were affected individuals notified?

A: Obviously, they won’t be noticed, which is one of the policies of Chinese dictatorship – “maintaining stability of the society”. They may be noticed from 3rd parties privately, or underground.

Q: What were the social, legal and ethical implications of the decisions made?

A: Of course, no. “Maintaining the stability of society” is the 1st priority of the Chinese Government’s policy, as it is a dictatorship.

On the final report, I have evaluated and suggested more security mitigation recommendations, comparing with the design proposal on Unit 6. More recommendations including:

1. The Linux kernel should always be updated, but not only relying on the patches and updates.

2. Make sure every unused port is blocked or turned off.

3. Unused web application, especially the CMS, should be uninstalled from the web server, to avoid any unnecessary ransomware or malware.

4. Encryption should always be used, such as SSL and SSH. Unencrypted ports and services like FTP and HTTP should be blocked, to avoid any injection, loss of data and leaking.
